What Affects the Cost?

1. Floor Condition

The most significant cost variable is the current state of your concrete. A clean, flat slab with no cracks or oil stains is quick to prep. But if your floor has multiple deep cracks, heavy oil penetration, or previous coatings that need grinding off, expect additional labor and material costs of $300–$800.

We always include a concrete inspection in our free estimate so there are never surprise charges on install day.

2. Coating Type

The system you choose dramatically affects the price. Standard epoxy is our entry-level option — reliable, beautiful, and more affordable. Polyaspartic coatings cost more because they use a premium material that cures faster and lasts longer. Metallic epoxy adds another layer of complexity and material cost.

  • Standard Epoxy Flake: Most affordable, full-day installation
  • Polyaspartic: 15–30% more expensive than epoxy but lasts significantly longer
  • Metallic Epoxy: Highest cost, most visually striking

3. Color and Flake Options

Standard color and flake combinations are included in our base pricing. Custom colors, high-density flake broadcast, or special effect blends may add $200–$500 to the project total. When we visit for your estimate, we'll bring a sample board with dozens of options so you can see exactly what you're getting.

4. Crack Repair

Minor hairline cracks are included in our standard prep. Structural cracks wider than ¼ inch, settlement cracks, or areas with significant spalling require additional repair work, typically $150–$400 depending on severity and length.

5. Square Footage

The bigger the floor, the lower the cost per square foot. A 600 sq ft three-car garage typically costs less per square foot than a 200 sq ft single-car garage because setup and mobilization costs are spread across a larger area.

6. Labor Costs in North Alabama

Huntsville's labor market is more affordable than major metros like Atlanta or Nashville, which is reflected in our pricing. You're getting the same high-quality polyaspartic materials used nationally, but at Alabama labor rates.

Is Epoxy Worth It for Huntsville Garages?

Short answer: yes. Here's why epoxy and polyaspartic coatings are particularly well-suited for Alabama garages:

Alabama Heat and Humidity

Huntsville summers are brutal — regularly over 90°F with high humidity. Standard concrete absorbs heat, moisture, and motor oil, leading to pitting and staining over time. A properly installed polyaspartic coating is fully UV-stable and won't yellow from sun exposure through south-facing garage doors. It also resists moisture vapor transmission, which is critical in our humid climate.

Durability

A well-installed epoxy or polyaspartic floor will last 15–20+ years with basic maintenance. That's significantly more value per dollar than paint (which lasts 1–3 years) or DIY store-bought kits (typically 2–5 years before peeling).

Resale Value

In competitive Huntsville neighborhoods like Hampton Cove, Jones Valley, and Madison's newer subdivisions, buyers increasingly expect finished garage floors. Real estate agents in our area tell us that an epoxy floor routinely returns 100–150% of its installation cost in added home value — and it helps homes sell faster.

Easy Cleaning

No more scrubbing oil stains or sweeping up concrete dust. An epoxy or polyaspartic floor cleans with a mop or pressure washer. Most of our customers say the cleaning time drops by 80% compared to bare concrete.
