How to Prepare Your Huntsville Garage for Epoxy Coating Installation (Complete Checklist)

By Rocket Floor Pros · February 2025 · 7 min read

Installation day is exciting — but it goes much smoother when the garage is ready when our crew arrives. Here's exactly what we ask homeowners to do before we show up, plus what you can expect throughout the day.

The good news: your preparation list is shorter than you'd think. Our crew handles all the hard work — diamond grinding, crack repair, coating application. Your job is mostly to clear the space and plan your day. Here's how to do it right.

The Night Before: Your Complete Prep Checklist

1. Clear the Entire Garage Floor

This is the single most important thing you can do. Every vehicle, every box, every bike, every shelf — anything that sits on or rolls across the floor needs to come out before our crew arrives. This includes:

  • All vehicles (cars, motorcycles, golf carts, ATVs)
  • Freestanding shelving units and workbenches (if they have legs that sit on the floor)
  • Any boxes, totes, or storage stacked on the floor
  • Bicycles, lawn equipment, sports gear
  • Any floor mats, anti-fatigue pads, or rugs

If you have wall-mounted shelving that doesn't touch the floor, you don't need to move it — but confirm with us during your estimate visit and we'll let you know if it will impact access.

2. Move Vehicles — and Plan Where to Keep Them

You'll need alternative parking for your vehicles during the installation and for 24–48 hours afterward (longer if you chose a standard epoxy rather than polyaspartic). We recommend:

  • Parking in your driveway or on the street the night before so you don't need to rush in the morning
  • Confirming with family members or roommates that they also can't use the garage until we give the clear
  • Arranging covered parking if rain is forecast (though the coating is on the floor, you'll want your car protected)

When you book your installation, we'll confirm exactly how long to plan for. Polyaspartic systems can often allow foot traffic same-day and vehicle parking within 24 hours. Standard epoxy typically needs 48–72 hours for vehicle parking.

3. Don't Clean or Mop the Floor Beforehand

This surprises a lot of homeowners. Our crews start every job with diamond grinding or shot blasting — a mechanical process that opens the pores of the concrete, removes surface contaminants, and creates the ideal profile for coating adhesion. If you mop with soap or use cleaning products the night before, you may actually leave residue that we then need to remove.

What you should do: Sweep up any loose debris, sand, or dirt. That's it. Don't mop. Don't scrub. Let us handle the concrete prep — it's what we're trained for.

If you have heavy oil stains, you're welcome to hit them with a degreaser 24–48 hours before — not the day of. Oil penetration into concrete can sometimes require additional prep time, and early treatment helps. For severe oil contamination, see our guide on whether your floor is ready for coating.

4. Check Your Garage Door and Ventilation

Our crew needs the garage door to open and stay open during the installation — both for access and for ventilation. Please verify:

  • Your garage door opener works and won't be locked or accidentally closed
  • You're not expecting packages or service deliveries that would require the garage to be sealed off
  • Any side entry doors to the garage are accessible (unlocked)

Polyaspartic and epoxy coatings have low VOC formulations, but ventilation is still important during the application and initial cure phase. We'll handle this, but a garage that can be fully opened helps.

5. Mark Anything You Want Us to Avoid or Address

If you have floor drains, specific cracks you're especially concerned about, areas with previous patches, or sections you want us to pay special attention to — mark them with painter's tape or note them when we arrive. We'll walk the floor with you before starting work to review the scope. This is the time to speak up.

What Happens on Installation Day

Understanding the installation sequence helps you know what to expect and when you can safely be in and around the garage. Here's our typical workflow:

Step 1: Floor Preparation (2–4 hours)

We start with diamond grinding or shot blasting the entire concrete surface. This is the loudest part of the day — it sounds like heavy industrial machinery (because it is). We open the surface profile, remove any existing sealers or coatings, and repair all cracks and chips. Don't try to be in the garage during this phase.

Step 2: Crack Repair and Detailing (30–60 minutes)

After grinding, we fill all cracks and joints with a flexible epoxy repair compound, feather it smooth, and allow it to cure before proceeding. This is also when we do any edge work or coving if included in your scope.

Step 3: Base Coat Application (1–2 hours)

The base coat goes down and we immediately broadcast the decorative flake (color chips) into the wet coating. We broadcast to rejection — meaning we throw enough chips that they're overlapping each other — for a consistent, full-coverage look. This is when your floor starts to look like a floor. You can watch from the doorway if you'd like.

Step 4: Scrape and Detail (30 minutes)

Once the base coat has tacked, we scrape off any excess standing flake, vacuum the floor, and edge-detail any areas the roller couldn't reach perfectly.

Step 5: Topcoat Application (1–2 hours)

The clear polyaspartic topcoat goes on last. This is the protective layer — it seals in the flake, provides the gloss, and delivers the durability and chemical resistance you're paying for. Once this is down, the floor needs to cure undisturbed.

Step 6: Walkthrough and Handoff

We'll walk the finished floor with you, point out any unique features (like control joint lines), go over the care instructions, and give you a written timeline for when you can walk on it, put items back, and park vehicles. You'll also receive your warranty documentation at this point.

After Installation: The First 72 Hours

Hours 0–6: Stay Off the Floor

The topcoat needs time to cure. Don't walk on the floor, drag anything across it, or allow animals in the garage during this window. Foot traffic at this stage can leave permanent impressions or scuff marks.

Hours 6–24: Light Foot Traffic Only

Most polyaspartic coatings can handle light foot traffic after 6–8 hours. You can walk on the floor with soft-soled shoes to retrieve items from the garage — but no wheeled carts, dropped tools, or heavy loads yet.

Hours 24–48: Return Light Items

After 24 hours, you can begin moving lighter items back into the garage — boxes, bicycles, hand tools. Set them down gently. Avoid dragging anything with sharp metal edges.

Hours 48–72: Vehicle Parking

Standard polyaspartic systems are typically ready for vehicle parking at 48–72 hours. We'll confirm your specific timeline based on the product used and the temperature during cure. In hot Alabama summers, curing often happens faster. In cooler months (below 50°F), it can take slightly longer.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Washing the floor with soap right before installation — leaves residue that affects adhesion
  • Leaving heavy furniture in the garage — we can't coat under it and it wastes your money
  • Turning off the HVAC — stable temperature (60–90°F) is ideal for coating cure
  • Letting pets in the garage after coating — pet hair embedded in wet topcoat is a real issue
  • Parking too early — the most common cause of premature hot tire pickup issues

Questions to Ask Before Installation Day

When we come out for your free estimate, feel free to ask:

  • What system are you installing — epoxy or polyaspartic?
  • Does it include a polyaspartic topcoat?
  • How many coats total?
  • How long before I can park a car on it?
  • Does the warranty cover hot tire pickup?
  • What happens if it rains during or after installation?

We answer all of these during every estimate visit. There should never be a surprise on installation day when you work with a professional crew that communicates clearly.
